When my son was diagnosed, I read everything I could find. Most of it was clinical and hard to translate into daily decisions. These three books were different. Each one changed how I thought about something important.

## Spastic Diplegia: Bilateral Cerebral Palsy
**Lily Collison**

[Buy on Amazon India](https://www.amazon.in/Spastic-Diplegia-Bilateral-Cerebral-Palsy-Understanding/dp/1952181003)

This is the book I wish I had from day one. Collison is a parent of a son with spastic diplegia who went on to study the condition in depth. The result is a comprehensive, plainly written guide that covers everything from understanding the motor problems to managing them across the lifespan.

It does not talk down to parents. It gives you the full picture and trusts you to work with it.

## Teaching Motor Skills to Children with Cerebral Palsy and Similar Movement Disorders
**Sieglinde Martin**

[Buy on Amazon India](https://www.amazon.in/TEACHING-MOTOR-SKILLS-Sieglinde-Martin/dp/1890627720)

Martin is a pediatric physiotherapist who also raised a child with cerebral palsy. The book is organized around how gross motor skills develop — head control, sitting, crawling, standing, walking — and gives you specific exercises for each stage.

What makes it useful is the photographs and the practical framing. It is not just theory. You can read a section and try something with your child the same day.

## The Boy Who Could Run But Not Walk
**Dr. Karen Pape**

[Buy on Amazon India](https://www.amazon.in/Boy-Could-Walk-Understanding-Neuroplasticity/dp/1988025052)

This one is about neuroplasticity — the brain's ability to rewire itself after injury. Dr. Pape, a neonatologist, makes the case that the window for improvement is longer than most parents are told, and that the right kind of practice can produce real change even in older children.

It is the most hopeful of the three. Read it alongside the other two to understand not just what to do, but why it matters to keep going.
